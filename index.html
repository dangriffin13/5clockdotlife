The gin & tonic was introduced by the army of the British East India Company in India. In the 1700s it was discovered that quinine could be used to prevent and treat malaria. The quinine was drunk in tonic water, however the bitter taste was unpleasant. British officers took to adding a mixture of water, sugar, lime and gin to the quinine in order to make the drink more palatable.
